## Version B (Full — 280 words)

Every bridge, every base, every water system that works when you turn the tap. Engineers. 🛠️

This week is National Engineers Week, and at SAME San Francisco Post, we don't just observe it. We live it year-round.

Our Post sits at the intersection of military engineering and the private sector. We connect Coast Guard, Army Corps, Navy, and Air Force civil engineers with the A/E/C firms who build alongside them. We pair seasoned PEs with students just finding their path. We host the site visits, forums, and events where those conversations happen in person.

Here's what that looks like in 2026:

⛳ Funding STEM scholarships through our Golf Tournament and Holiday Gala
🏗️ Hosting site visits and technical forums connecting military and civilian engineers
⚓ This March, co-hosting an Industry Day with U.S. Coast Guard Civil Engineering Unit Oakland. Registration details dropping soon.

National Engineers Week started in 1951, around the same time our Post was getting its footing here in the Bay Area. George Washington, a surveyor and military engineer himself, would appreciate the connection.

To every engineer in our network: from the field offices at CEU Oakland to the design firms across the Bay, from the cadets to the professors at USF. This week is yours. 🇺🇸

How did you get into engineering? Tell us your story in the comments.
