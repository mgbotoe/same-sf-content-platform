## Version B (Full — 240 words)

"We Build, We Fight." For 84 years, that's exactly what the Seabees have done.

Happy Birthday to the U.S. Navy's Naval Construction Battalions, established March 5, 1942. Born out of necessity after Pearl Harbor, the Seabees were the answer to a simple question: who builds the infrastructure when the construction site is a war zone?

The answer? Construction workers, engineers, electricians, equipment operators, and tradespeople who raised their hands and shipped out. They built airfields in the Pacific in days, not months. They repaired bridges while taking fire. They turned remote coastlines into operational bases.

84 years later, that mission continues. Seabees deploy to build, repair, and sustain military installations and humanitarian projects around the world. They train alongside the communities they serve. And they carry forward a legacy that connects the construction industry to national defense in a way few other units can match.

At SAME San Francisco Post, that connection is personal. Our members include military engineers, civilian contractors, and A/E/C professionals who understand what it means to build something that matters. The Seabee ethos runs through everything we do.

To every Seabee, past and present: 84 years of "Can Do." Here's to many more. 🇺🇸

Know a Seabee? Tag them below and wish them a happy birthday.
