## Version B (Full)

This is what it looks like when military and industry show up in the same room.

On March 5, the Lemoore Field Chapter of the SAME San Francisco Post hosted a Lunch and Learn and 84th Seabee Birthday celebration at Naval Air Station Lemoore. More than 25 members and guests came from across the Central Valley, representing Public Works Department Lemoore, NAVFAC SW, Harper Construction, Bush Construction, Fresno State University, Vanir Construction Management, Fleet Readiness Center-West, and Renewable Solar Inc.

Greg Woods, Managing Director of Renewable Solar Inc (RSI) and a retired Navy Civil Engineer Corps Commander, kicked things off with a presentation on heat-related issues and the methods RSI uses to build resilient solar power systems. With a career spanning both military engineering leadership and private-sector energy, Mr. Woods brought exactly the kind of perspective that makes our mission real: connecting government and industry professionals around shared challenges.

After the presentation, the chapter honored a tradition that goes back decades. The oldest and youngest active-duty Seabees in the room, Command Master Chief Zetino from Fleet Readiness Center West and ENS Wed Al Nuaimi, Assistant Public Works Officer for NAS Lemoore, cut the 84th Seabee Birthday cake together. It's a simple moment, but it carries the full weight of the Naval Construction Force's history and the people who continue that legacy today.

This event happened because people like CDR Sam Lee, PE (Public Works Officer, NAS Lemoore) and the Lemoore Field Chapter volunteers put in the work to make it happen. Thank you.

And we're not slowing down. The Lemoore Field Chapter is heading to Fresno State next to welcome new Student Chapter leadership. From active-duty installations to university campuses, this is how our Post grows.
