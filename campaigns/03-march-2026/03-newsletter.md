## SECTION 3: April Monthly Meeting — Geospatial Tools Panel

**Header bar** (navy blue #003478 background, white text): April Meeting: Geospatial Tools for A/E/C & Asset Management

**Image**: MemberPlanet event flyer (Trimble banner graphic)

Join us for a forward-looking panel discussion on the role of geospatial technologies in transforming the A/E/C and infrastructure industries. This session will highlight how tools such as LiDAR, GNSS, reality capture, and digital twins are driving smarter decisions across the full project lifecycle, from planning and design to construction and long-term asset management.

Featuring experts from **Trimble**, with invited participation from public agencies, the panel will share real-world applications, lessons learned, and emerging trends shaping the future of infrastructure delivery.

Attendees will gain practical insight into how these technologies are being deployed in the field today, along with a clearer understanding of where the industry is headed.

The evening includes a **buffet-style dinner, one drink per guest**, and dedicated time for networking with professionals across engineering, surveying, and construction disciplines.

## SECTION 4: Spotlight — Lemoore Field Chapter Lunch & Learn + Seabee Birthday

**Header bar** (navy blue #003478 background, white text): Lemoore Field Chapter: Lunch & Learn + 84th Seabee Birthday

**Hero image**: Photo from the Lemoore event (cake cutting or group photo if available)

**Hosted at Naval Air Station Lemoore | March 5, 2026**

On March 5, the Lemoore Field Chapter of the SAME San Francisco Post brought together more than 25 members and guests for an afternoon of professional development and military tradition at Naval Air Station Lemoore.

**The presentation:**
Greg Woods, Managing Director of Renewable Solar Inc (RSI) and a retired Navy Civil Engineer Corps Commander, delivered a presentation on Heat Related Issues and the methods RSI uses to build resilient solar power systems. With experience spanning both military engineering leadership and private-sector energy, Mr. Woods brought a perspective that connects directly to our mission of bridging government and industry.

**The celebration:**
Following the presentation, the chapter celebrated the 84th birthday of the U.S. Navy's Construction Force, the Seabees. In a time-honored tradition, the oldest and youngest active-duty Seabees in attendance cut the birthday cake together: Command Master Chief Zetino from Fleet Readiness Center West and ENS Wed Al Nuaimi, Assistant Public Works Officer for NAS Lemoore.

**Who was there:**
The event drew attendees from Public Works Department Lemoore, NAVFAC Southwest, Harper Construction, Bush Construction, Fresno State University, Vanir Construction Management, Fleet Readiness Center-West, and Renewable Solar Inc.

*Thank you to CDR Sam Lee, PE (Public Works Officer, NAS Lemoore) and the Lemoore Field Chapter for putting this together.*

**What's next for the Lemoore chapter?** They're heading to Fresno State to welcome new Student Chapter leadership. Stay tuned for details.
