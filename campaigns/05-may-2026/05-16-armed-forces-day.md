## Version B (Full — 280 words)

Every day of the year, someone in uniform is doing a job most people will never see. Standing a midnight watch. Running a convoy. Maintaining an airfield thousands of miles from home. Today, we honor them. 🇺🇸

Armed Forces Day falls on the third Saturday of May, and it carries a specific purpose: recognizing the men and women currently serving in the U.S. Armed Forces. Not those who have served before (that's Veterans Day). Not those who made the ultimate sacrifice (that's Memorial Day). Today is about the active-duty, Reserve, and Guard members on the job right now.

At SAME San Francisco Post, military-civilian collaboration isn't an idea. It's what we do. Our members include active-duty engineers from the Army Corps, Coast Guard, Navy, and Air Force, alongside the A/E/C professionals who design and build federal infrastructure beside them. That partnership shows up in our site visits, our Industry Days, and our mentorship of young professionals and STEM students.

The uniformed engineers in our network bring something our Post can't function without: firsthand knowledge of what the military needs built, maintained, and defended. The civilian engineers bring the technical depth and industry experience that turns those requirements into reality. Together, that's the SAME mission.

To every service member currently wearing the uniform, whether you're stationed at a Bay Area installation or deployed overseas: your work matters, and we're grateful for it.

Happy Armed Forces Day from SAME San Francisco Post.

How are you honoring Armed Forces Day? Tag a service member in the comments. 🇺🇸
