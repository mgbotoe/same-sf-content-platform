## Version A (Short — 180 words)

Two hundred fifty-one years. The U.S. Army is older than the nation it defends. 🇺🇸

On June 14, 1775, the Continental Congress authorized the creation of the Continental Army, a full year before the Declaration of Independence. That same date would later become Flag Day, making June 14 one of the most meaningful days on the American calendar.

For SAME San Francisco Post, the Army's birthday hits close to home. The Army Corps of Engineers has been a cornerstone of our Post since its founding. Our members work alongside Army engineers on infrastructure, flood control, environmental restoration, and construction projects across the Bay Area and beyond.

From the Revolutionary War to today's installations and civil works, the Army has always been in the business of building, not just fighting. That's a mission we understand.

Happy 251st Birthday to the United States Army. And happy Flag Day to all who honor the colors.

## Version B (Full — 310 words)

Before there was a United States, there was an Army. 🇺🇸

On June 14, 1775, the Continental Congress created the Continental Army to fight for a nation that didn't yet exist. 251 years later, the U.S. Army remains the backbone of American defense, the largest branch, and one of the most consequential engineering organizations in the world.

That last point matters to us at SAME San Francisco Post. The U.S. Army doesn't just deploy soldiers. It deploys engineers. The Army Corps of Engineers plans, designs, and builds critical infrastructure, from levees and dams to military installations and disaster response systems. Here in the Bay Area, USACE has been central to flood risk management, navigation, ecosystem restoration, and some of the most complex civil works in the country.

Our Post exists because of the relationship between military engineers and the civilian A/E/C community. Army engineers have been part of that equation from the beginning. They sit on our boards, attend our events, lead our site visits, and mentor the next generation of engineering professionals through our Young Professionals and scholarship programs.

June 14 is also Flag Day, the anniversary of the Continental Congress adopting the Stars and Stripes in 1777. Two observances, one date, both rooted in the idea that some things are worth building and defending.

To every soldier serving today, and to every Army veteran in our SAME SF community: 251 years of service. 251 years of building what the country needs.

Happy Birthday, Army.
