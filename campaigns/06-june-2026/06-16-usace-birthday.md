Two days after the Army's birthday, we celebrate the branch that put "engineer" in military engineering. Happy 251st Birthday to the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers. 🇺🇸

USACE was established on June 16, 1775, just two days after the Continental Army itself. From its first mission of building fortifications during the Revolution, the Corps has grown into one of the largest public engineering organizations in the world.

For SAME San Francisco Post, USACE isn't just a partner. It's at the core of why we exist. Our mission is to bring military and civilian engineers together, and no organization represents that intersection more directly than the Army Corps.

Here in the Bay Area, USACE manages flood risk along the Sacramento-San Joaquin Delta, maintains navigation channels in San Francisco Bay, leads ecosystem restoration at Hamilton Wetlands, and supports military construction at installations across the Western Pacific.

Our members have toured USACE projects, collaborated on federal construction, and built professional relationships through the Corps that span decades.

251 years of building what the nation needs. That's a record worth honoring.

## Version B (Full — 340 words)

"Essayons." Let us try. For 251 years, the U.S. Army Corps of Engineers has done far more than try. 🇺🇸

Established June 16, 1775, USACE is one of the oldest organizations in the federal government and one of the largest public engineering agencies in the world. The Corps plans, designs, builds, and operates infrastructure that millions of Americans depend on every day: locks, dams, levees, harbors, military installations, environmental restoration sites, and disaster response systems.

At SAME San Francisco Post, the Army Corps is central to our identity. SAME was founded on the principle that military and civilian engineers are stronger when they work together. USACE is where that principle becomes reality. Corps officers and civilian employees sit alongside A/E/C professionals at our events, on our committees, and at our leadership tables.

In the Bay Area, the Corps' footprint is significant:

- Flood risk management across the Sacramento-San Joaquin Delta
- Navigation and dredging operations in San Francisco Bay
- Ecosystem restoration, including Hamilton Wetlands and South San Francisco Bay
- Military construction supporting installations throughout the region and Western Pacific

Our Post has toured USACE projects, hosted Corps leaders at our forums, and connected young professionals with Army engineers who've become mentors and colleagues. Those relationships don't happen by accident. They happen because SAME builds the bridge between uniform and industry.

251 years ago, the Continental Congress created a Corps of Engineers to build fortifications for a new nation. Today, USACE manages over $80 billion in civil works and military construction annually, employs more than 37,000 people, and operates in over 130 countries.

That's not just engineering. That's a mission.

Happy 251st Birthday, USACE. Here's to the next chapter.
