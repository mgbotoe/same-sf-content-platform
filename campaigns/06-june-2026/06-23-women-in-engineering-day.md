## Version B (Full — 370 words)

Behind every piece of infrastructure you trust, there's an engineer. And increasingly, that engineer is a woman.

Today is International Women in Engineering Day, a day to recognize the women making their mark across every corner of the profession, from military installations to municipal water systems, from federal construction sites to research labs.

At SAME San Francisco Post, this day is personal. Our community includes women who serve as military engineers, lead A/E/C firms, manage multimillion-dollar construction projects, and mentor the students who'll follow them. They're not hypothetical role models. They're our members, our board leaders, and our colleagues.

We're also investing in the pipeline. Our Post awards STEM scholarships to students pursuing engineering degrees, with an intentional focus on building a profession that looks more like the communities it serves. Our Young Professionals program, led by members like Isabel Andaya, EIT, creates space for early-career engineers to build networks, attend site visits, and connect with established professionals who remember what it felt like to be new in this industry.

Here's the reality: women earn about 24% of engineering bachelor's degrees in the U.S. but hold only about 16% of engineering jobs. That gap doesn't close on its own. It closes when organizations like SAME create the events, the mentorship, and the professional relationships that help women stay and advance in engineering.

That's what we do. Not once a year. Every month. At every event. Through every connection.

If you're a woman in engineering or a young professional considering this field, SAME SF wants you at the table. Our next event is a chance to meet military and civilian engineers who are building careers and building infrastructure in the Bay Area.

To the women in our SAME SF community who show up, lead, and build: today is yours, and every other day, too.
