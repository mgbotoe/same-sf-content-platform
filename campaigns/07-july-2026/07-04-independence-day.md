## Version B (Full — 280 words)

Before he was Commander in Chief, George Washington was a surveyor and military engineer. He mapped terrain, planned fortifications, and understood something fundamental: wars aren't won by soldiers alone. They're won by builders.

Happy 250th Independence Day. 🇺🇸

Two hundred and fifty years ago, a group of colonists declared independence and then had to build an entire nation from scratch. Roads, bridges, forts, harbors, supply lines. The Continental Army's engineers laid the groundwork, and we haven't stopped building since.

That legacy runs through every branch of the military. The Army Corps of Engineers has shaped our rivers, coastlines, and cities for over two centuries. The Navy Seabees built airfields under fire across the Pacific. Air Force Prime BEEF and RED HORSE squadrons keep our bases operational worldwide. The Coast Guard's civil engineering teams maintain the shore infrastructure that protects our waterways. Every branch, every generation, building what the mission demands.

At SAME San Francisco Post, our members carry that same mission forward. We are military engineers, civilian architects, A/E/C professionals, and students who believe that the connection between national defense and the built environment matters. We build together, we learn together, and we serve together.

On this 250th anniversary of American independence, we honor the engineers, soldiers, and builders who made this country possible, and the ones still at it today.

From SAME San Francisco Post: Happy Fourth of July to the builders, the planners, the constructors, and every American who serves. 🇺🇸
