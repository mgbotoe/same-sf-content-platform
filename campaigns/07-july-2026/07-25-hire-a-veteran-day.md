## Version B (Full)

A veteran walks into a job interview with years of leadership under pressure, technical problem-solving in tough environments, and a track record of delivering results with limited resources.

That's not a candidate who needs a chance. That's a candidate who brings an advantage.

Today is National Hire a Veteran Day.

In architecture, engineering, and construction, veteran talent isn't just welcome. It's a natural fit. Military engineers manage large-scale construction projects, coordinate logistics across multiple sites, and make critical decisions under time pressure and resource constraints. Combat engineers, Seabees, RED HORSE airmen, and Coast Guard civil engineering teams bring direct construction and infrastructure experience. And the leadership, discipline, and accountability that come with military service? Those show up on day one.

At SAME San Francisco Post, this connection between military service and the A/E/C profession is the reason we exist. Our events bring together military engineers, government agencies, and private-sector firms — and SAME national runs programs specifically designed to support this transition:

**SAME Career Transition Workshop (CTW)**
A 3-day event that pairs transitioning service members with A/E/C recruiters. Resume reviews, one-on-one interviews, professional headshots, and direct access to firms hiring veterans. The 2026 workshop connected 86 transitioning service members with 108 recruiters.
same.org/event/ctw

**SAME Career Center**
Job board with positions posted by firms across the A/E/C industry. Search roles, post your resume, and access career coaching resources.
same.careerwebsite.com

Here's how you can make today count:

- **Employers**: Post veteran-friendly roles on the SAME Career Center and attend our next networking event
- **Veterans in transition**: Check out the CTW and Career Center. These exist because of you
- **Industry professionals**: If you've hired a veteran who made your team better, tell that story today

The military builds leaders. The A/E/C industry needs them. SAME connects the two.
