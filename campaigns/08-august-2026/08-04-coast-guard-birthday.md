## Version B (Full — 320 words)

The oldest continuous seagoing service in the United States turns 236 today.

Happy Birthday to the U.S. Coast Guard, established August 4, 1790. ⚓

Most people know the Coast Guard for search and rescue, maritime law enforcement, and disaster response. But behind every cutter, every air station, and every coastal facility, there's a civil engineering mission that keeps it all running.

Coast Guard civil engineers design, build, and maintain shore infrastructure across the country: air stations, housing, waterfront facilities, aids to navigation, and the specialized structures that support operations in some of the most demanding environments in the federal portfolio. It's a mission that requires the same technical expertise and project delivery skills found across the A/E/C industry.

At SAME San Francisco Post, we've seen that connection firsthand. In March 2026, we co-hosted the USCG Industry Day alongside Civil Engineering Unit Oakland, bringing together Coast Guard engineering leaders and Bay Area architecture, engineering, and construction firms. The event gave private-sector professionals a direct look at the Coast Guard's infrastructure priorities, project pipeline, and partnership opportunities.

That kind of event is exactly why SAME SF exists. Our mission is to build connections between military engineers and the private sector, and the Coast Guard's civil engineering program is one of the strongest examples of why those connections matter.

From the lighthouses that guided ships in the 1800s to the modern facilities that support today's operations, Coast Guard engineers have been building essential infrastructure for more than two centuries.

To every Coast Guardsman, past and present: thank you for 236 years of keeping our waters safe. Semper Paratus.

Interested in working with the Coast Guard's civil engineering program? Connect with SAME San Francisco Post to stay informed about upcoming events and industry opportunities.
