## Version B (Full — 180 words)

Twenty-five years.

On September 11, 2001, 2,977 people lost their lives. First responders, 343 firefighters among them, gave everything they had. In the hours and days that followed, military engineers and civilian volunteers mobilized to clear wreckage, restore critical infrastructure, and support a city and a nation in crisis.

The engineers at Ground Zero worked in conditions no textbook prepares you for. They assessed structural damage in real time, coordinated debris removal operations that lasted months, and helped stabilize the surrounding buildings and utilities. Across the country, military installations activated and communities came together.

Twenty-five years later, the buildings at Ground Zero stand again. The infrastructure has been rebuilt. But the loss remains.

At SAME San Francisco Post, our members are engineers, architects, and constructors who build the infrastructure that keeps communities safe. On this anniversary, that mission feels especially personal.

We remember the lives lost. We honor those who responded. And we continue to build with purpose.

September 11, 2001. Never forget.
