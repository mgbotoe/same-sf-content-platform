## Version B (Full — 310 words)

Before there was a Constitution, before there was a flag, there was a Navy. On October 13, 1775, the Continental Congress voted to fit out two armed vessels, and the United States Navy was born.

Happy 251st Birthday to the U.S. Navy. ⚓

Two and a half centuries of service. From the wooden hulls of the Revolutionary War to the carrier strike groups operating across the globe today, the Navy has defended American waters, projected strength overseas, and maintained freedom of the seas through every era of the nation's history.

For those of us in the military engineering community, the Navy holds a special place. The Naval Construction Battalions, the Seabees, have carried the "We Build, We Fight" mission since 1942. They build airfields, repair ports, and construct the infrastructure that sustains operations in every theater. NAVFAC, the Naval Facilities Engineering Systems Command, manages billions of dollars in military construction, shore infrastructure, and environmental programs each year.

At SAME San Francisco Post, that work is personal. Our members design, engineer, and build naval facilities along the Pacific coast and beyond. Seabee veterans sit alongside civilian engineers at our events. The relationship between the Navy's construction mission and our A/E/C community is one of the strongest threads in our Post's history.

Steven "Sid" Osgood, P.E., PMP, F.SAME, our Past President, has spent his career at the intersection of military service and engineering leadership. His example reflects what our Post is built on: professionals who serve and professionals who build, working side by side.

To every Sailor serving today and every veteran who wore the uniform: 251 years of protecting the nation. Fair winds and following seas.
