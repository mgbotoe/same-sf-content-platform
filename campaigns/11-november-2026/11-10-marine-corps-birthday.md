## Version B (Full — 340 words)

Semper Fidelis. Always Faithful. For 251 years, those words have defined a branch of service like no other.

Happy 251st Birthday to the United States Marine Corps. 🇺🇸

On November 10, 1775, the Second Continental Congress resolved to raise two battalions of Marines at Tun Tavern in Philadelphia. What started as a small fighting force has become one of the most respected military institutions in the world, with a culture of discipline, adaptability, and mission focus that runs from recruit to general.

Within the Corps, engineers carry a tradition of their own. Marine combat engineer units, including the 1st Combat Engineer Battalion at Camp Pendleton, handle construction, demolition, route clearance, and obstacle breaching under the most difficult conditions any builder will ever face. When the mission calls for a road, a bridge, or a fortification in a combat zone, Marine engineers answer.

That engineering mission is something our SAME San Francisco Post members understand well. Military and civilian engineers in our network design, build, and maintain the installations and infrastructure that support Marine operations across the Pacific and beyond.

Our connection to the Marine Corps goes beyond the professional. Every December, our Holiday Gala fills the ballroom at the Marines' Memorial Club in San Francisco. The Club itself is a tribute to the Marines and service members who fought in the Pacific Theater during World War II. Walking through its halls, past the photos and memorabilia, is a reminder of the sacrifices that make our work meaningful.

And at each Gala, our members carry forward another Marine tradition: Toys for Tots. The Marine Corps Reserve program has been putting gifts in children's hands since 1947, and SAME SF is honored to collect donations in support of that mission year after year.

To every Marine serving today and every veteran of the Corps: 251 years of Always Faithful. Happy Birthday.
