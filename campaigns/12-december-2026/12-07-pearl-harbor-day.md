## Version B (Full — 190 words)

Eighty-five years ago today, the attack on Pearl Harbor killed 2,403 Americans, sank or damaged 21 ships, and destroyed 347 aircraft. In a single morning, the United States was at war.

December 7, 1941. A date which will live in infamy.

What followed was one of the largest military mobilizations in history. Among the most urgent needs: engineers who could rebuild what was destroyed and build what the Pacific campaign demanded. In March 1942, the Navy established the Naval Construction Battalions, the Seabees, in direct response to the attack. Military engineers expanded airfields, repaired harbors, and constructed forward operating bases across the Pacific Theater.

That work did not end in 1945. The military engineering mission born from Pearl Harbor carries forward in every base, every installation, and every infrastructure project our armed forces depend on today.

At SAME San Francisco Post, we serve a community of military and civilian engineers whose work traces back to that mission. Eighty-five years later, the commitment remains the same.

We remember those lost. We honor those who answered the call. We never forget.
