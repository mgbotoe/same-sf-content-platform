## Version B (Full — 280 words)

Before there was a United States, there was the militia.

Happy 390th Birthday to the National Guard. 🇺🇸

On December 13, 1636, the Massachusetts Bay Colony organized its militia companies into the North, South, and East regiments. That decision created the oldest military component in what would become the United States. Three hundred and ninety years later, the National Guard remains the foundation of our nation's defense at home and abroad.

Today, more than 440,000 soldiers and airmen serve in the Army National Guard and Air National Guard across all 50 states, three territories, and Washington, D.C. They deploy overseas alongside active-duty forces. They respond to natural disasters and emergencies at home. And they do both with a readiness that comes from training year-round while also serving their communities as civilians.

For engineers, the Guard holds a special place. Guard engineer units are often the first to respond when disasters strike on American soil. During floods in the Midwest, wildfires in California, and hurricanes along the Gulf Coast, Guard engineers have cleared debris, repaired critical infrastructure, built emergency access routes, and restored essential services. That work is military engineering at its most immediate and its most personal.

At SAME San Francisco Post, we serve a community where military readiness and civilian infrastructure meet. The Guard's dual mission of national defense and domestic response reflects what we do every day: connecting the people who design, build, and protect the infrastructure our nation depends on.

To every National Guard soldier and airman, past and present: thank you for 390 years of service to community and country.

Always Ready, Always There.
