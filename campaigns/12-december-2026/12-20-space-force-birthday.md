## Version B (Full — 300 words)

The newest branch of the U.S. military turns seven today. In that short time, the Space Force has already redefined what military readiness looks like.

Happy 7th Birthday to the U.S. Space Force. 🇺🇸

On December 20, 2019, the United States Space Force was established as the sixth branch of the armed forces, the first new military branch since the Air Force separated from the Army in 1947. Its creation recognized what defense leaders had long understood: space is a domain that demands dedicated focus, capability, and people.

Today, the Space Force operates satellite constellations, monitors threats in orbit, manages launch operations, and provides the space-based capabilities that every other branch depends on, from GPS to missile warning to secure communications.

Behind every one of those missions is infrastructure. Launch complexes at Vandenberg and Cape Canaveral. Satellite tracking stations spanning the globe. Ground-based radar and communication installations that have to be engineered for precision and resilience. The Space Force may look up, but its foundation is built on the ground.

That is where the military engineering community comes in. Designing and constructing facilities for space operations means working at the intersection of advanced technology and proven construction practice. It means adapting to new requirements while maintaining the reliability that every mission depends on.

At SAME San Francisco Post, we are watching this story take shape. Our members include engineers and constructors who work on the facilities that support space operations. As the Space Force grows, so does the engineering challenge, and our community is part of meeting it.

To every Guardian serving today: happy 7th Birthday. The mission is new, but the engineering tradition behind it is generations deep.

Semper Supra. Always Above.
