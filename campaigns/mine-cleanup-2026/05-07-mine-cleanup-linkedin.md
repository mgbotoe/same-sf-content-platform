## Version B — Full

For the first time, EPA Region 9 and the SAME San Francisco Post are bringing practitioners together specifically to talk about abandoned mine cleanup.

The First Abandoned Mine Cleanup Information Exchange is June 16, 2026 — 9:00 AM to 1:30 PM at the EPA Region 9 Office in San Francisco. This isn't a broad environmental conference. It's a focused technical forum for people working CERCLA hardrock mine sites: the residuals, the terrain, the communities, the design constraints that don't appear in textbooks.

The day covers three topic areas:
- Residential mine cleanup projects
- Geomorphic and natural reclamation-focused repository design and construction
- Large-scale mine waste excavation and consolidation in remote and challenging terrain

We're accepting abstracts now. EPA Region 9 and SAME are looking for 6 presentations (20 minutes each) and up to 12 posters. If you've got lessons learned, emerging approaches, or practical design solutions worth sharing, this is where they belong.

**Abstract deadline: May 19.**

Registration opens May 18 and is limited to 3 attendees per firm — presenters and poster contributors count toward that limit.

Abstract link in the comments. Questions? Reach out.
