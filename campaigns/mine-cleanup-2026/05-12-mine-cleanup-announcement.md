**body_copy**:

Hardrock abandoned mine sites present a specific set of challenges: remote terrain, highly variable waste materials, cleanup designs that have to integrate with natural landscapes and the communities around them. The work is technically demanding, and the lessons practitioners are learning on these sites don't have many places to land.

EPA Region 9 and the SAME San Francisco Post are building that space.

The first Abandoned Mine Cleanup Information Exchange is June 16, 2026 at the EPA Region 9 Office in San Francisco. A half-day technical forum for practitioners working CERCLA hardrock mine sites, the day includes presentations, poster sessions, facilitated discussions, and networking, focused on three topic areas:

- Residential mine cleanup projects
- Geomorphic and natural reclamation-focused repository design and construction
- Large-scale mine waste excavation and consolidation in remote and challenging terrain

**We're looking for presenters and poster contributors.** EPA Region 9 and SAME are accepting 6 presentations (20 minutes each) and up to 12 posters. If your work touches any of the three topic areas, submit an abstract. **Deadline: May 19.**

Registration opens May 18. Each firm registers once and selects up to 3 attendees. Presenters and poster contributors count toward your firm's spots.
