## Event Description (copy-paste ready — plain text only, no markdown)

EPA Region 9 and the SAME San Francisco Post are hosting the first Abandoned Mine Cleanup Information Exchange — a collaborative forum for practitioners working CERCLA hardrock mine sites.

Hardrock abandoned mine sites present challenges distinct from other CERCLA sites: highly variable waste materials, large volumes, difficult terrain, and the need to integrate engineered remedies with natural landscapes and surrounding communities. The practitioners doing this work have underscored the importance of adaptive design, constructability-focused planning, and transparent information sharing — and the need for continued learning and collaboration across the cleanup community.

This Information Exchange is built around that need. The forum brings practitioners together to:

• Share lessons learned
• Discuss emerging best practices
• Explore practical design and construction solutions specific to hardrock abandoned mine cleanup

The event features facilitated technical discussions, presentations, poster sessions, and networking focused on three topic areas:

• Residential mine cleanup projects
• Geomorphic and natural reclamation-focused repository design and construction
• Large-scale mine waste excavation and consolidation in remote and challenging terrain
