# SAME SF Brand Voice & Tone Guide

## Voice Profile

SAME SF sounds like a respected colleague who serves alongside you — someone who's proud of the work without being boastful, serious about the mission without being stiff, and genuinely invested in the people behind the projects.

## Tone Spectrum

| Context | Tone | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Event promotion | Energetic, inviting | "What makes SAME SF's Golf Tournament the most fun golf event of the year?" |
| Military observances | Respectful, measured | "Today we pause to remember Pearl Harbor and honor the 2,400 Americans who lost their lives." |
| Sponsor recognition | Grateful, specific | "For many years, ECC has stood beside the SAME San Francisco Post as a dedicated supporter." |
| Member spotlights | Warm, celebratory | "Sid's connection to SAME runs deep. His father, a SAME Fellow, first brought him to Post meetings in high school." |
| Scholarship content | Purposeful, hopeful | "Supporting future leaders in science and engineering isn't just a program — it's our purpose." |
| Community resources | Direct, helpful | "Many in our community continue to feel the effects of the government shutdown. SAME SF is sharing local resources." |
| Throwback posts | Storytelling, nostalgic | "Long before our Holiday Gala filled the Marines' Memorial Club ballroom, SAME SF members gathered under open skies." |

## DO

- Open with a hook, not a label ("What an evening!" not "Event Recap:")
- Name specific people, companies, and places
- Use "we" and "our" — write as the community, not about it
- Include concrete details (dollar amounts, attendance numbers, scholarship counts)
- End with a clear CTA on promotional posts
- Use short paragraphs (2-3 sentences max for LinkedIn)
- Write like you're talking to a peer at a networking event

## DON'T

- Start posts with "We are excited to announce" or "We are proud to share"
- Use corporate jargon ("synergy", "leverage", "optimize", "stakeholder alignment")
- Write walls of text — LinkedIn rewards scannable content
- Use more than 3 emojis per post (zero on solemn observances)
- Use any word or phrase from the exclusion list (see `exclusion-list.md`)
- Write in passive voice ("The event was attended by..." → "Over 80 golfers joined us...")
- Be vague ("a great event" → "an evening of connection, celebration, and live music")
- Use em dashes (—) in LinkedIn posts
- Use transactional sponsor language ("generous donation", "funded", "couldn't do this without their money")
- Post the same content on the same day across channels
- Repost LinkedIn content within 2-4 weeks

## Proven High-Performing Formulas (from 82-post Voice Analysis)

### Formula 1: Meaning + Moment + Community (highest engagement)
Open with *what it means*, not what happened. Share a specific moment or reflection. Name people and transitions. Close with forward energy.
> "The Holiday Gala is always more than a night out in the city. It's a reminder of why our Post exists."

### Formula 2: Scene + Purpose + Gratitude
Lead with sensory detail, connect to the mission, give specific thanks, end with a soft CTA.
> "What an evening! Thank you to everyone who joined us at Headlands Brewing..."

### Formula 3: Then + Now + Bridge
Historical anchor with sensory language. Bridge with "decades later that same spirit..." Connect to current mission.
> "Long before our Holiday Gala filled the ballroom, SAME SF members gathered under open skies."

### Formula 4: Recognition + Context + Impact
Enthusiastic announcement, specific achievements with metrics, personal dimension, engagement invitation.
> "Meet Steven 'Sid' Osgood... Sid's connection to SAME runs deep."

**Key insight**: High performers lead with *meaning*. Low performers lead with *what happened*.

## Phrases to Steal (proven high-engagement)

- "More than just a [event], it was..."
- "The common thread has been people."
- "That's the heart of this [event]..."
- "Isn't just a program, it's our purpose."
- "This is what [value] looks like."

## Sponsor Language Rules

Frame sponsors as **partners**, not funders.

| DO say | DON'T say |
|---|---|
| "Partnership in..." | "Generous donation" |
| "Support enabled..." | "Funded" |
| "Commitment to..." | "Couldn't do this without their money" |
| "Together with..." | "Thanks for the check" |

## Post Structure Templates

```
[Hook line — make them stop scrolling]

[2-3 sentences: what, when, where, why it matters]

[Bullet details: date, time, location, special guests]

[CTA: registration link or action]

[3-5 hashtags]
```

### Military Observance
```
[Observance statement — factual, respectful]

[1-2 sentences connecting to SAME SF's mission]

[Closing line of honor/remembrance]

[2-3 hashtags — no emojis]
```

### Event Recap
```
[Energetic opening — reaction, not summary]

[2-3 highlights with specifics (names, numbers, moments)]

[Thank sponsors/partners by name]

[Forward-looking close or CTA]

[3-5 hashtags]
```

### Member/Sponsor Spotlight
```
[Name and role introduction]

[Their story — how they connect to SAME SF]

[Specific contribution or achievement]

[What they represent for the community]

[3-5 hashtags]
```

## LinkedIn Best Practices (SAME SF Specific)

- **Best performing content types**: Photo carousels, event recaps, scholarship posts
- **Top engagement drivers**: Holiday Gala content, member spotlights, golf tournament live posts
- **Optimal post length**: 150-300 words for standard posts, up to 500 for major recaps
- **Posting frequency**: 1-2 posts/week baseline; max 3/week during campaigns
- **Hard cap**: 12 posts/month. Beyond this, engagement drops 45% (247 B2B pages, 18-month study)
- **Best posting days**: Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday (primary). Friday morning is secondary (for recaps). Avoid weekends (45%+ engagement drop).
- **Best posting time**: **7:00 AM Pacific, every post.** Consistency yields 6.1% engagement vs 4.2% for random timing. 7 AM PT also hits East Coast at 10 AM ET (their peak window).
- **First 60 minutes are critical**: Engagement velocity in the first hour determines whether LinkedIn shows the post to 2nd/3rd-degree connections. Respond to every comment within 15 minutes. Alert board members to engage immediately after posting.
- **Minimum gap**: 24+ hours between posts. LinkedIn algorithm promotes only one post per account per 24-hour window.
- **Links**: Put registration/external links in **first comment**, not post body (algorithm penalizes outbound links)
- **End posts with a genuine question** to drive comments (comments = 15x weight of likes in LinkedIn algorithm)
- **Top format**: Carousels/documents generate 2-3x more dwell time and ~6x more engagement than text posts
- **Employee advocacy**: Company page reach dropped 60-66% in 2024-2026. Personal profiles reach 561% more. When board members (Scott, JR, Madina, Isabel) reshare from personal profiles, the post reaches 5x+ more people. Coordinate reshares after posting.

## Output Rules

Every content-generating skill MUST produce:
1. **Version A**: Short/punchy (150-250 words)
2. **Version B**: Full/detailed (300-500 words)
3. **Alt phrasing**: 1 alternative opening or angle

This gives the user options without extra prompting.

## Audience-Specific Tone

Adjust tone based on primary audience:

| Audience | Approach | CTA Style |
|---|---|---|
| **Sponsors** | Appreciation, ROI, visibility, partnership framing | Moderate: "Let's continue building together" |
| **Students/Youth** | Inspirational, encouraging, opportunity-focused | Soft: "Learn more about our programs" |
| **DoD Partners** | Professional, technical, collaboration/resilience focus | Moderate: "Join our next briefing" |
| **Members** | Balanced, civic-minded, highlight their contributions | Varies by content |
| **General** | Warm, mission-forward, inviting | Strong: "Register now" / "Join us" |

## CTA Intensity Levels

| Level | When to Use | Examples |
|---|---|---|
| **Soft** | Observances, recaps, spotlights | "Learn more about our mission" / No CTA at all |
| **Moderate** | Mid-campaign, community posts | "Save your spot" / "Connect with us" |
| **Strong** | Last-call, registration deadlines | "Register now" / "Only 3 days left" / "Don't miss this" |

Never use strong CTAs on solemn content. Never use soft CTAs on final registration pushes.

## Sensitivity Protocol

**PAUSE AND CONFIRM** before generating content about:
- Tragedies, disasters, or loss of life
- Government shutdowns or political events
- Solemn observances (Memorial Day, Pearl Harbor, 9/11)
- Any event involving controversy or sensitive topics

Ask the user: "This is sensitive content. Before I draft, can you confirm the tone and any specific details to include or avoid?"
