# SAME SF Event Campaign Sequence Templates

## Campaign Philosophy

Every major event gets a **multi-post sequence** that builds anticipation, drives registration, covers the event live, and recaps afterward. The sequence follows a narrative arc — not just repeated "register now" posts.

**Campaign Rules (research-backed):**
- **Never post 2 promotional posts back-to-back.** Interleave with value content (spotlights, throwbacks, stories).
- **Max 3 posts/week** even at peak campaign intensity — this is total posts, not campaign-only.
- **Hard cap: 12 posts/month total** across all content types.
- **Put registration links in the first comment**, not the post body (algorithm penalizes outbound links).
- **Vary the angle** with each post — different value proposition, not the same CTA repeated.
- **60% of campaign posts should be value-oriented** (stories, spotlights, throwbacks) with the event woven in as context.

## Sub-Deadline Rule — Mandatory at Campaign Intake

Any deadline **inside** a campaign (abstract submissions, early bird pricing, registration opens, scholarship deadline) is its own content objective. It gets its own mini-sequence planned at intake — before any post is written.

**During campaign intake, ask:** *"Are there any deadlines inside this campaign beyond the event date?"*
Each one maps to a mini-sequence with dates assigned immediately.

| Sub-deadline type | Minimum posts | Standard sequence |
|---|---|---|
| Call for abstracts / presentations | 3 | Open → Mid-run (2 wks before close) → Last call (1 wk before close) |
| Early bird pricing | 2 | Opens → 48-hr reminder |
| Registration opens | 2 | Opens post → 1-week reminder |
| Scholarship / nomination deadline | 3 | Announce → Mid-run → Last call |

**Runway rule:** The first post in a sub-deadline sequence should go out no later than **4 weeks before the deadline** when possible, and 2 weeks minimum. If the form/link isn't live yet, draft the post and hold it — don't skip the sequence.

**What went wrong with Mine Cleanup abstracts (May 2026):** The abstract form opened ~4 weeks before the May 19 deadline but the campaign brief captured it as a data point, not a content objective. Result: only 2 posts, first going out 2 weeks before close. The correct plan would have been: announce open in mid-April → mid-run reminder ~May 5 → last call May 13.

## Campaign Tier System

Every event falls into a tier based on size, significance, and registration friction. The tier determines runway length, number of posts, and the promotion phases used.

### Tier 1: Flagship Annual Events (Gala, Golf Tournament)
- **Runway**: 8-10 weeks
- **Total posts**: 8-10 (spread across ~10-12 weeks including recap)
- **Phases**: Launch → Ramp Up → Convert → Event → Recap
- **Campaign posts per week**: ~1 (leaves room for regular content alongside)
- **Post-event posts**: 2-3 (recap, sponsor thank-you, impact/scholarship story)

### Tier 2: Mid-Size Events (Industry Days, Site Visits, Member Appreciation)
- **Runway**: 3-4 weeks
- **Total posts**: 2-4
- **Phases**: Announce → Drive → Recap
- **Campaign posts per week**: ~1

### Tier 3: Small/Free Events (Happy Hours, Volunteer Posts, Meetups)
- **Runway**: 1-2 weeks
- **Total posts**: 1-2
- **Phases**: Announce → Optional Recap

### The Three Promotion Phases (Research-Backed)

| Phase | Timing | Purpose | Content Style |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Launch** | 8-10 weeks out (Tier 1) or 3-4 weeks (Tier 2) | Awareness. Save the date. Build anticipation. | TBTs, sponsor spotlights, "why this matters" — mostly value content |
| **Ramp Up** | 4-6 weeks out (Tier 1) or 2-3 weeks (Tier 2) | Interest. Program details. Registration nudge. | Program reveals, testimonials, behind-the-scenes — mix of value and promotional |
| **Convert** | 1-2 weeks out | Urgency. Last call. FOMO. | Countdown, final push, "spots filling up" — promotional but earned by prior value |

### Weekly Posting Budget

Campaign posts share the same weekly slots as regular content — they don't get extra posts on top of baseline.

| Week Type | Typical Mix | Total |
|---|---|---|
| Quiet week | 1 observance or 1 TBT | 1 |
| Normal week | Observance + TBT or engagement post | 2 |
| Campaign week | 1 campaign post + 1 regular post | 2 |
| Busy/collision week | 1 campaign + 1 observance + 1 TBT or engagement | 3 (max) |

**When collisions happen** (campaign post + observance + TBT in same week):
1. Observances are non-negotiable — post day-of, period
2. Campaign posts flex — bump by a day or shift to next week
3. TBTs and volunteer posts are most flexible — skip or shift

### Campaign Post Types (Content Mix)

Not every campaign post is a "register now." The best campaigns use value-first content:

| Post Type | Content Mix Category | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Save the date / announcement | Promotional | "Mark your calendar: Holiday Gala, Dec 10" |
| TBT to last year's event | Value | "Last year's Gala raised $X for scholarships..." |
| Sponsor spotlight | Value / Partnership | "Thank you to ECC for 10+ years of support" |
| Program/speaker reveal | Thought leadership | "This year's keynote: [speaker] on [topic]" |
| Scholarship/impact story | Value | "Meet [student], our 2025 scholarship recipient" |
| Registration reminder | Promotional | "Registration is open — early bird ends [date]" |
| Last call / countdown | Promotional | "Two days. Have you registered?" |
| Recap with photos | Value | "What a night! Highlights from the Gala..." |
| Sponsor/volunteer thank-you | Value | "None of this happens without [sponsors]" |

TBTs and sponsor spotlights serve double duty: they're value content that builds toward the event without feeling like ads. This is how you keep the 60/25/15 content mix healthy even during heavy campaign months.

## Annual Golf Tournament Campaign (6-8 weeks)

### Phase 1: Announce (6-8 weeks out)
1. **Save the Date** — Date, venue, purpose (scholarship fundraiser)
2. **Sponsor Reveal** — Thank confirmed sponsors with tier callouts
3. **Throwback** — Historical golf tournament photos + legacy stats

### Phase 2: Build (4-6 weeks out)
4. **Why Attend** — What makes this different (networking, scramble format, lunch, prizes)
5. **Beginner Welcome** — "New to golf tournaments? Perfect, this one's for you."
6. **Early Bird Reminder** — Pricing deadline push
7. **Volunteer Call** — Behind-the-scenes appreciation + volunteer CTA

### Phase 3: Drive (1-2 weeks out)
8. **Last Call Registration** — Urgency, specific remaining spots
9. **Cross-Post** — Partner events in same week (e.g., Sacramento Post forum)
10. **Final Sponsor Thank-You** — Updated sponsor list with all tiers

### Phase 4: Event Day
11. **Live from the Course** — Real-time photos, energy, highlights
12. **Mid-Day Update** — Candid shots, fun moments, contest results

### Phase 5: Recap (1-2 weeks after)
13. **Thank You Recap** — Highlights, stats, photo album link
14. **Impact Statement** — Dollars raised, scholarships funded, community impact

## Annual Holiday Gala Campaign (8-10 weeks)

### Phase 1: Announce (8-10 weeks out)
1. **Save the Date** — Date, venue (Marines' Memorial Club), purpose
2. **Throwback: Last Year's Gala** — Photos + highlights from previous year

### Phase 2: Build (5-7 weeks out)
3. **Scholarship Spotlight** — Previous recipients, what the Gala funds
4. **Sponsor Reveal** — Prior year sponsors + invitation for new sponsors
5. **Registration Open** — Link, pricing, what's included
6. **Then & Now Carousel** — Gala through the decades

### Phase 3: Engage (3-4 weeks out)
7. **Speaker/Program Preview** — Who's speaking, special moments planned
8. **USF Partnership** — Relationship with scholarship recipients
9. **Toys for Tots Tie-In** — Season of giving + bring a toy
10. **Thanksgiving Gratitude Post** — Community appreciation

### Phase 4: Drive (1-2 weeks out)
11. **Two Days Away** — Final details, excitement, last registration push
12. **Fun Detail** — Bulldogs, music, venue highlights
13. **Sponsor Thank-You (Updated)** — Full sponsor list with tiers

### Phase 5: Event & Recap
14. **Live from the Gala** — Real-time photos, atmosphere
15. **Presidential Remarks Recap** — Key quotes, leadership transition
16. **Sponsor Thank-You Recap** — Specific impact of sponsorship
17. **Scholarship Recipient Feature** — Individual student stories
18. **Year-End Wrap** — What the Gala means to the Post

### Phase 1: Announce (3-4 weeks out)

### Phase 2: Drive (1 week out)
3. **Last Call** — Registration reminder, limited spots
4. **Partner Cross-Post** — Tag host organization/city officials

### Phase 3: Recap
5. **Event Recap** — Photos, highlights, technical details, key takeaways
6. **Partner Thank-You** — Thank host, special guests, attendees

### Phase 1: Announce (2-3 weeks out)
1. **Save the Date** — Casual tone, what to expect
2. **Who Should Come** — Member types, bring-a-guest incentive

### Phase 2: Drive (1 week out)
3. **Reminder** — Details, RSVP link, what's included (food, swag, prizes)

### Phase 3: Recap
4. **Thank You Recap** — Photos, connections made, community highlights

## Free/Small Event Campaign (3-4 weeks) — Industry Days, Happy Hours, Meetups

For free events with lower registration friction:

### Phase 1: Announce (3-4 weeks out)
1. **Save the Date / Announcement** — What, where, when, why it matters. Can be a teaser mention inside another post (e.g., Engineers Week mentioning an upcoming Industry Day) or a standalone post.

### Phase 2: Drive (2-3 weeks out)
2. **Full Details + Registration** — When registration link is live. Photorealistic venue graphic or real photo. All logistics.

### Phase 3: Last Call (2-3 days out)
3. **Last Call** — Urgency. "This Thursday." Short, punchy.

### Phase 4: Recap (3-7 days after)
4. **Event Recap** — Real photos, highlights, what attendees learned. Tag people.

**Key insight**: Most RSVPs come in the **last 2 weeks** regardless of when promotion starts. Don't stress low early numbers — that's normal. The "last call" post typically drives the most registrations.

## Newsletter/Email Frequency

- **Baseline**: Monthly newsletter (45% of nonprofits do this — it's the standard)
- **With events**: Monthly + 1 event reminder = 2/month. This is the sweet spot for nonprofits with content.
- **Max**: ~5 emails/month during heavy campaign periods. More than that causes fatigue.
- **Best send day/time**: Tuesday-Thursday, 10 AM in primary audience timezone
- **Event reminder emails**: Send 1-2 weeks before major events as a separate, focused email (not bundled into the newsletter)
- **Consistency matters more than frequency**: A reliable monthly newsletter beats sporadic bursts

## Email Campaign Templates (Mailchimp)

### Event Invitation Email
- **Subject line**: Keep under 50 chars, create urgency or curiosity
- **Header**: Event name + date + compelling one-liner
- **Body**: 3-4 short paragraphs — what, why, who, how
- **CTA button**: "Register Now" or "Save Your Spot"
- **Footer**: SAME SF branding, social links, unsubscribe

### Event Reminder Email
- **Subject line**: "X days until [Event]" format
- **Body**: Quick highlights + logistics (time, parking, dress code)
- **CTA button**: "Confirm Your RSVP" or "Get Directions"

### Post-Event Thank You Email
- **Subject line**: "What a night!" or similar warm opener
- **Body**: Highlights, stats, photos, next event teaser
- **CTA button**: "View Photos" or "Save the Date for [Next Event]"

### Newsletter/Monthly Update Email
- **Subject line**: Monthly theme or top highlight
- **Sections**: Upcoming events, recent highlights, member news, sponsor spotlight
- **CTA buttons**: Per section (register, read more, get involved)
