### Banned Structural Patterns

**Pompous copula substitutes** — never replace "is" with:
- "serves as"
- "stands as"
- "marks" (e.g., "this marks a pivotal moment")
- "represents" (when "is" works)

**The "Here's" family** — announces content instead of delivering it:
- "Here's the thing"
- "Here's the kicker"
- "Here's the deal"
- "Here's the reframe"
- "Here's the trap"
- "Here's why this matters"
- "Here's what that looks like in practice"
- "But here's the thing —" (even mid-paragraph)

**Negative parallelism** — false profundity via reframe:
- "It's not X — it's Y."
- "Not X. Not Y. Just Z."
- "The question isn't X. The question is Y."

**Rhetorical self-questions** — asking then immediately answering:
- "The result? Devastating."
- "The worst part? Nobody saw it coming."

**Filler transitions** — signal nothing, cut them:
- "It's worth noting..."
- "It bears mentioning..."
- "Notably,"
- "Importantly,"
- "Interestingly,"

**Teacher mode** — condescending hand-holding:
- "Let's unpack..."
- "Let's explore..."
- "Let's break this down..."
- "Let's dive in..."
- "Think of it as..."
- "It's like a..."
- "Quick reframe" / "Quick sidebar" / "Quick note"
- "Step back for a second"

**Performed sincerity** — reads as fake:
- "And honestly,"
- "Honestly,"

**Conclusion signals** — competent writing doesn't announce it's ending:
- "In conclusion,"
- "To sum up,"
- "In summary,"

**The dismissal formula** — acknowledges problems only to wave them away:
- "Despite its challenges..."
- "Despite these challenges, [optimistic ending]"

**Superficial -ing tails** — tacking false significance onto a sentence:
- "...highlighting its importance"
- "...reflecting broader trends"
- "...contributing to the region's rich cultural heritage"
- "...underscoring its role as..."

## What To Use Instead

Write plainly and specifically. Instead of:
- "This event made a significant impact" -- "This event raised $12,000 for STEM scholarships"
- "Our community continues to evolve" -- "We added 15 new members this quarter"
- "A game-changing approach" -- "A new format that doubled attendance"
- "Navigating challenges" -- "Responding to the federal shutdown"
