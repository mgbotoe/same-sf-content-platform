## Posting Guidelines by Observance Type

### Solemn Observances (Memorial Day, Pearl Harbor, 9/11)
- No emojis
- Factual, respectful tone
- Focus on sacrifice and remembrance
- Brief — don't over-write
- End with a simple tribute line — **NO closing question**
- Never say "Happy Memorial Day" — these are NOT celebrations
- A question after a tribute reads as engagement-fishing on a day that isn't about engagement

### Celebratory Observances (Branch Birthdays, Armed Forces Day, Independence Day, Veterans Day)
- 1-3 emojis max (flag, cake, branch-related)
- Veterans Day: honorific tone but celebratory — recognizing service, not mourning loss
- Highlight branch contributions to engineering/infrastructure
- Connect to SAME SF mission
- Include the founding fact or ordinal year — even in short posts, one concrete fact earns the post
- Can include fun facts or history
- Tag relevant service members in our network
- Branch-specific emoji guide: 🎓 scholarships, 🇺🇸 observances, ⚓ Coast Guard, ✈️ Air Force, 🪖 Army
- **End with a genuine question** — connects to the community, invites comments (15x algorithmic weight of likes)

### National Observances (MLK Day, Independence Day)
- Connect to SAME SF's values (service, community, equity)
- MLK Day: Highlight leadership, service, and community impact — tie to SAME SF's mission of building bridges across sectors
- Respectful but not solemn — these are days of reflection AND action
- 1-2 emojis max
- **End with a genuine question** — reflection-oriented, not promotional

### Professional Observances (Engineers Week, Women in Engineering Day, Hire a Veteran Day)
- Focus on SAME SF programs that support the cause
- Highlight members or partners in that space
- Include the founding/establishment fact — always an opportunity to educate
- Include CTA (join SAME, attend an event, connect with us)
- **End with a genuine question** — invites the community to share their experience
